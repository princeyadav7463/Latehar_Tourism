Suga Bandh Waterfalls - Latehar

Suga Bandh Waterfalls, located near the village of Baresand in the Latehar district of Jharkhand, is one of the region's hidden natural gems. It lies close to the Palamu Tiger Reserve and is about 60 kilometers from Betla.

Natural Beauty

Surrounded by dense forests and rolling hills, the Suga Bandh Waterfall cascades beautifully during the monsoon season. The waterfall flows into a natural basin, making it a perfect picnic and relaxation spot for locals and tourists alike.

Accessibility

The site is easily accessible by road from Betla, Latehar, and nearby villages. Although it's lesser-known, its proximity to Palamu Tiger Reserve and Netarhat makes it a worthy addition to any travel itinerary in the region.

Best Time to Visit

  • Season: Monsoon and post-monsoon (July to November) when the waterfall is in full flow
  • Ideal for: Nature photography, family outings, scenic views, and peaceful picnics

Nearby Attractions

  • Betla National Park – 60 km away
  • Netarhat – Around 70 km away
  • Baresand Village – Nearby base point

Whether you are a nature lover or simply looking to escape into the serene landscapes of Jharkhand, Suga Bandh Waterfalls offers a refreshing experience. Don't forget your camera!
