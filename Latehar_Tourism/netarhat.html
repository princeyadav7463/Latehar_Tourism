Netarhat – Queen of Chotanagpur

Netarhat is a scenic hill station located in Latehar district in the Indian state of Jharkhand. Known as the "Queen of Chotanagpur", it is one of the highest points of the Chotanagpur plateau and is famed for its breathtaking landscapes, waterfalls, and forest-covered hills.

Geography

Situated at a height of 1,128 meters (3,701 ft), Netarhat lies on a plateau in the Pat region of Chotanagpur. The area is about 6.4 km long and 4 km broad. It’s covered with thick forests of Sal, Kendu, Mahua, and Eucalyptus trees, and the terrain features crystalline rocks topped with laterite.

Education

Netarhat is also home to the renowned Netarhat Vidyalaya, a residential public school established in 1954.

Tourism

Netarhat is surrounded by natural wonders and popular sightseeing spots. Some of the key attractions include:

  • Lodh Falls – Highest waterfall of Jharkhand, 70 km from Netarhat.
  • Upper Ghaghri Falls – 4 km away, surrounded by dense forest.
  • Naina Falls – Scenic natural spot nearby.
  • Mahuadanr Wolf Sanctuary – India’s only sanctuary dedicated to wolves.
  • Magnolia Point – 10 km from Netarhat, famous for sunset views.
  • Sunrise Point – A perfect spot to enjoy the morning sunrise.
  • Lower Ghaghri Falls – 10 km from Netarhat.
  • Koel River View Point – Just 3 km away.
  • Suga Bandh Falls – A peaceful picnic destination 70 km away.
  • Sadni Falls – Located 35 km from Netarhat.
  • Betla National Park – 94 km away, part of the famous Palamu Tiger Reserve.
  • Pear Orchards & Pine Forests – Forest department’s green initiatives.
  • Lake Park & Sunrise Park – Family-friendly local attractions.
  • 90° Fall – A dramatic waterfall and scenic viewpoint.

Transport

Netarhat is located 156 km west of Ranchi, 118 km from Daltonganj, and 82 km from the Latehar district headquarters. Roads are the primary means of connectivity.
